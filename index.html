London Fire Brigade Visualization

From fire to ashe.

Motivation

We have chosen this dataset in order to investigate the patterns of fire incidents, and since London Fire Brigade in one of the largest and most busy fire departments in the globe, we figured this would be a great place to start.
The dataset covers the years 2013, 2014 and 2015, as well as the first quarter of 2016.
We shifted to this project from our previous since we found that the data was corrupted and our preliminary analysis could not find anything worth discussing.

Basic stats

The set is comprised of 26 colomns/parameters that we can put to the test, including:

  • time
  • Date
  • Location
  • Type of incident
  • Property type
  • easting/northing location pointer
Beneath is a series of inital visualisations to show some basic features of the data.

Year Data

This plot shows the total number of incidents for every year during the period supplied. This was to see whether there was a change in incidents during the period. This does not seem the case. The number of incidents for 2016 in naturally smaller since we are only a quarter through the year, datawise.

Month data

This plot shows the total number of incidents divided into months for all the three years combined. No months really stand out amoungst the crowd, although it does seem like there is a tendensy towards more fires happening in the beginning of the year. We have no theory on this.

Date of month data

This plot shows the total number of incidents divided into each day of the month over the three years. The data seems evenly divided on the days, except for the 31st of the month. This i due to the fact that only half the months have a 31 days.

Time slot data

This plot shows the total number of incidents over the year, divided onto timeslots. This means that a fire happining at 14:30 will be in the 14-15 timeslot. Here is a much clearer tendensy. Fires are at their lowest during the night and early morning and clearly more frequent around supper. this is possibly becuase this is the times where you would cook with fire.
